# Cost Per Wear (CPW) — Complete Guide

Cost per wear (CPW) is a metric used to measure the true value of a clothing item based on how often it is worn.

It is one of the most effective ways to evaluate wardrobe efficiency, reduce waste, and make better purchasing decisions.

---

## What is Cost Per Wear?

Cost per wear is calculated by dividing the purchase price of a garment by the number of times it has been worn.

### Formula

Cost per wear = Purchase Price ÷ Number of Wears

---

## Example

- Jacket price: €200  
- Worn: 20 times  

Cost per wear = €200 ÷ 20 = €10 per wear

If the same jacket is worn 100 times:

Cost per wear = €200 ÷ 100 = €2 per wear

---

## Why Cost Per Wear Matters

Most people evaluate clothing based on price, not usage.

Cost per wear shifts the focus from:
- “How much did this cost?”
to:
- “How much value am I getting from this?”

### Benefits

- Identifies underused clothing
- Encourages smarter buying decisions
- Reduces wardrobe waste
- Improves long-term value
- Supports sustainable fashion habits

---

## High vs Low Cost Per Wear

### Low CPW (good)
- Frequently worn items
- Versatile clothing
- Core wardrobe pieces

### High CPW (inefficient)
- Rarely worn items
- Trend-based purchases
- Poor fit or styling mismatch

---

## What Affects Cost Per Wear?

Several factors influence CPW:

- Frequency of use
- Versatility (can it be styled multiple ways?)
- Seasonality
- Quality and durability
- Personal style alignment

---

## Common Mistakes

- Buying cheap items that are rarely worn
- Keeping unused clothing for too long
- Ignoring fit and comfort
- Not tracking usage

---

## How to Reduce Cost Per Wear

- Wear existing items more often
- Build outfits around core pieces
- Avoid impulse purchases
- Track what you actually wear
- Remove or resell unused clothing

---

## Cost Per Wear and Sustainability

Cost per wear is directly linked to sustainability.

Higher usage means:
- Less overconsumption
- Lower environmental impact
- More efficient wardrobes
