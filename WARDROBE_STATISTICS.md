# Wardrobe Statistics (2026)

This page provides key statistics about clothing usage, wardrobe behavior, and fashion consumption patterns.

These insights highlight inefficiencies in how people use their wardrobes.

---

## Key Statistics

- The average person wears only 20% of their wardrobe regularly
- 80% of clothing is underused or rarely worn
- A typical wardrobe contains €1,000–€3,000 worth of clothing
- A significant portion of this value is unused

---

## Clothing Usage Patterns

Most wardrobes follow a similar pattern:

- A small number of items are worn frequently
- The majority of items are worn rarely
- Some items are never worn after purchase

---

## Financial Impact

Unused clothing represents locked value.

Examples:
- €200 jacket worn twice = €100 per wear
- €50 shirt worn 50 times = €1 per wear

This shows that cheaper items can provide more value if used more frequently.

---

## Behavioral Factors

Common reasons for underused clothing:

- Impulse purchases
- Poor fit or comfort
- Trend-based buying
- Lack of outfit planning

---

## Sustainability Impact

Low usage leads to:
- Increased waste
- Overproduction
- Higher environmental impact

Improving wardrobe usage is one of the most effective ways to reduce fashion waste.

---

## The Role of Cost Per Wear

Cost per wear helps measure efficiency.

Higher usage leads to:
- Lower cost per wear
- Better financial value
- More sustainable consumption
